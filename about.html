About Us...

“Injustice anywhere is a threat to justice everywhere”

Martin Luther King

Law foundation is non-for-profit organisation, carrying out grassroot level interventions since 2016 under the Criminal Justice Fellowship Programme of Tata Institute of Social Sciences, Mumbai and registered as a Trust in 2019.Our core work revolves around enabling socio-legal aid, networking, training, rehabilitation, re-integration and advocacy with respect to the custodial, institutional, legal and constitutional rights of socio-economically marginalized communities. To this end, permission to work with the criminal justice system and interact with persons detained or confined in prisons and government residential institutions in Bihar has been obtained from the Bihar District Legal Service Authority and Hon'ble Chief Justice Mr. Iqbal Ahmad Ansari & Mr. Rajendra Menon.

Mission

To extend socio-legal aid to disadvantaged communities, groups and individuals; enabling equal access to constitutional rights.

Vision

Facilitating access to justice.

Our Board Members

Maja Daruwala

Maja Daruwala is currently Senior Advisor at CHRI, particularly focussing on the Access to Justice Programme. She was Director of CHRI for twenty years, until September 2016.

Maja Daruwala is the Chief Editor of India Justice Report and had worked in India, England, Singapore and Sri Lanka. She sits on several charitable boards including the International Women’s Health Coalition, NAMATI, International Record Management Trust, Public Affairs Centre (PAC) Bangalore. She is a recipient of the Nani Palkiwala Award for protection and preservation of civil liberties in India.

Prof. Vijay Raghavan

Vijay Raghavan is a Professor in the Centre for Criminology and Justice, School of Social Work, Tata Institute of Social Sciences, Mumbai.

Prof. Vijay Raghavan heads Prayas, TANDA, KOSHISH field action projects of TISS which work on rights and rehabilitation of criminal Justice affected populations and nomadic tribes. He has done a Masters in Social Work with specialization in Criminology and Correctional Administration and PhD focused on Social Work and Criminology from Tata Institute of Social Sciences. He has more than three decades of experience of working with youth offenders and legal aid and access to justice for under trial prisoners. He has also been a part of numerous public interest litigations on under trials and women rescued from commercial sexual exploitation and member of various committees appointed by the government and courts to suggest measures to address problems faced by prisoners and under trials. He has published eight books and have twenty-one articles to his credit.

Prof.Sangmitra Sheel Acharya

Sanghmitra Sheel Acharya is a Professor in the Centre of Social Medicine and Community Health, School of Social Sciences, Jawaharlal Nehru University, New Delhi.

Prof. Sanghmitra Sheel Acharya was the first Director of Indian Institute of Dalit Studies, New Delhi (2015-18). She has been a Visiting Fellow at CASS, China; Ball State University, USA and UPPI, Manila, The Philippines; East West Center, Honolulu, Hawaii and University of Botswana. She was awarded Asian Scholarship Foundation fellowship in 2005. She has, chaired sessions, given keynote addresses in national and international conferences. She has published extensively in peer reviewed journals on the issues of health and social exclusion with focus on youth in development; gender in urban spaces; and North East India. Her current work is on social discrimination in health care access among cleaners (sanitation workers). She has published three books and have thirty articles to her credit.

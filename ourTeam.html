Who we are

In 2016, Mr. Praveen Kumar initiated socio-legal aid services in Bihar under the Criminal Justice Fellowship Program of the Tata Institute of Social Sciences, Mumbai. In the course of his fellowship, he intervened in several prisons and addressed issues of socio-legal aid. After a sustained endeavor of three years of extending socio-legal aid services to the custodial population lodged in various jails in Bihar, he met Santosh Kumar and Shubhendu Shekhar during his fellowship journey. They all encountered several issues pertaining to the disadvantaged custodial population. Thus, they registered LAW Foundation as a trust in 2019 whose core work revolves around the areas of socio-legal aid services, networking, training, research, and documentation, besides advocacy of custodial, institutional, legal, and constitutional rights of the custodial population. It is also focused on the rehabilitation and re-integration of socioeconomically marginalized and vulnerable individuals and groups.

Mr. Praveen Kumar
Founder and Director
Mr. Praveen Kumar serves as the Director and Founder of the organization, overseeing fundraising, project delivery, and strategic initiatives. His journey began as a Criminal Justice Fellow at the TISS Centre for Criminology and Justice (2016–19) under Prof. Vijay Raghavan's mentorship, focusing on issues affecting under-trial inmates in Bihar. Recognized for his grassroots work, he was awarded the Praxis Fellowship in 2019. Praveen Kumar has authored numerous publications in journals such as Economics & Political Weekly, Taylor & Francis (Routledge), and SAGE, addressing topics including caste dynamics, the criminal justice system, and challenges faced by under-trial prisoners. His commitment remains steadfast in advocating for marginalized groups deprived of equitable justice.
Shubhendu Shekhar
Co-Founder
Shubhendu Shekhar is the co-founder of the organization and leads the research, project operation, team members, and communication. He has completed his B.A. LL.B. from National Law University, Jodhpur, and has been a part of this organization since its inception. He completed his LLM from TISS, Mumbai, in Access to Justice. He has recently been awarded the Chevening Awards Scholarship and the Felix Scholarship. He has been working continuously on the issues of marginalized communities who are imprisoned and are unable to secure due justice. He has several publications in his name on caste, the criminal justice system, and issues of under-trial prisoners in Economics & Political Weekly, Taylor & Francis (Routledge), SAGE, and editorial pieces.
Adv. Santosh Kumar
Senior Counsel
Santosh Kumar, the Senior Counsel at our organization, is a dedicated advocate committed to addressing socio-legal issues impacting disadvantaged communities, Notified and Denotified Tribes in Bihar. With a robust background, Santosh completed his LL.B from Patna University and holds a Master's degree in Rural Development. He specializes in the Bihar Excise Act, securing 15 notable acquittals and actively providing free socio-legal aid services to over five hundred vulnerable inmates across the state. His passion for justice is further demonstrated through his role in conducting training programs on various aspects of the law, making significant contributions to community empowerment and legal education.
Shalini
Legal Aid Advocater
Shalini is an advocate with an LL.M. in Intellectual Property Rights (I.P.R.) and serves as a dedicated law officer at the organization. Since 2021, she has played a pivotal role in managing administrative responsibilities while leading the organization's socio-legal aid project. She specializes in criminal law, domestic violence cases, and family matters, advocating for fair trials and securing over 15 acquittals. With a strong commitment to community empowerment and legal education, Shalini actively engages with communities to raise awareness about their rights and duties. She has appeared in approximately 250 cases, successfully obtaining bail in nearly 200 instances. Shalini's passion lies in creating impactful change by ensuring justice and upholding the rights of the marginalized.

Md. Quadir Ali
Head of Research and Development Center
Md. Quadir Ali is a Research Fellow in the Department of Psychology at Christ University, Bangalore, with ongoing doctoral research. Presently, he has been the Head of the Research and Development Centre (RDC) of the LAW Foundation since October 2023, working remotely on orchestrating the comprehensive operations of the RDC, including the design and execution of research projects, adherence to rigorous methodologies, and the pursuit of funding opportunities. He is instrumental in shaping the structure and content of reports and papers, ensuring the highest standards of academic and research excellence, targeting expanding research domains, forging strategic collaborations, and enhancing policy advocacy efforts, with a strong emphasis on integration, community engagement, and global outreach for impactful and equitable reforms within society. Additionally, his interests revolve around criminal psychology, scale construction, dream analysis, and social psychology. He has been the Article Editor for the Scopus-indexed journal Sage Open since May 2022 and in peer-reviewer roles in the Scopus-indexed journals, i.e., Journal of Gender Studies, Cogent Education, Sage Open, and Cogent Social Sciences since March 2021.

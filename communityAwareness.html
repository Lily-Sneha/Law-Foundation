Community Awareness: Legal Awareness Program in Community with CPLVs

LAW Foundation runs a community awareness program on constitutional values and legal rights through Community Para-legal Volunteers (CPVs). The program aims to empower marginalized communities by creating awareness about their legal rights and the importance of constitutional values.

The CPLVs are community members who are trained by LAW Foundation to provide legal awareness and aid services in their communities. They act as a bridge between the community and the legal system, providing information on legal rights and helping community members to access legal aid services.

The program focuses on educating communities on constitutional values such as equality, justice, and liberty. This helps to create a deeper understanding of the importance of these values in their daily lives and how they can be used to assert their rights.

The program also provides information on legal procedures and rights, including those related to land rights, labor rights, and women's rights. The CPVs work with community members to identify legal issues and provide assistance in addressing these issues.

The program has been successful in creating awareness among marginalized communities about their legal rights and the importance of constitutional values. It has also helped to build trust between the legal system and the community, leading to increased access to justice and legal aid services.

In conclusion, the community awareness program on constitutional values and legal rights through CPVs is an important initiative by LAW Foundation. It empowers marginalized communities by creating awareness about their legal rights and the importance of constitutional values. The program has been successful in building trust between the legal system and the community, leading to increased access to justice and legal aid services.

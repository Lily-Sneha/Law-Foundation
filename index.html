Our Story

"Our organisation was envisaged during the post-graduation years of our founder, Mr. Praveen Kumar when he visited the district prison of Kalyan, Maharashtra as a part of his field visit while pursuing his MA in Social Work, Criminology and Justice from TISS, Mumbai. He conducted a preliminary research and comprehended the importance of working within Criminal Justice Systems in his home State of Bihar. He was awarded a Criminal Justice Fellowship, TISS when he shared his idea with his mentor Prof. Vijay Raghavan. He initiated the work in 2016 and consequently received permission from the Chief Justice of Patna High Court Justice Iqbal Ahmed Ansari and Justice Rajendra Menon, to work inside the prison. Further, he collaborated with our co-founder Mr. Shubhendu Shekhar and Mr. Santosh Kumar to commence the organisation 'LAW Foundation".

What We Do

Socio-Legal Aid Services

Socio-legal-aid is an intervention model that claims to restore the principles of Justice, Equality, and Equity in the Criminal Justice System in India. The organization is committed to ensuring that every individual, regardless of their socio-economic background, has access to justice and legal assistance. The custodial population, which includes prisoners, detainees, and under-trial prisoners, often face several challenges and barriers when it comes to accessing legal aid services and counseling. The LAW Foundation recognizes these challenges and works to bridge the gap by providing these populations with legal aid services, counseling, and support. The socio-legal aid services provided by the organization include legal representation, legal advice, and assistance in filing petitions and appeals. The organization also conducts legal awareness programs and workshops for the custodial population, which helps them understand their rights and obligations under the law.

Socio-Legal Counselling

In addition to legal aid services, the LAW Foundation also provides counseling and support services to the custodial population. The organization understands that the custodial population faces a range of emotional and psychological issues, including stress, anxiety, and depression. Therefore, it provides counseling services to help individuals cope with these issues and develop a positive outlook towards life. The organization also conducts vocational training programs and provides job placement assistance to the custodial population, which helps them reintegrate into society and lead a normal life after their release.

Community Health Services

LAW Foundation through its RRR program believes that Release from jail of any person is not the end in the criminal justice system. The Reintegration & Rehabilitation Program allows the released prisoners to leave their prison life behind and start a new path. The purpose of this programme is to assist the prisoners in identifying their potential and build their self-respect. This lowers their risk of reoffending and facilitates a smooth transition back into society. We all know that spending time in jail can be extremely distressing and negatively impact an inmate's mental health. This programme attempts to make convicts self-sufficient, demonstrate the good elements of life, and open their eyes to greater possibilities in order to rehabilitate and mould their brains.

Legal Literacy

LAW Foundation conducted a legal literacy program in collaboration with government schools in Patna to promote legal awareness and education among children. The program aimed to educate children about their legal rights and responsibilities in a fun and interactive manner. As a part of the program, the organization organized an easy writing competition among children on various legal topics such as the Constitution of India, fundamental rights, and the importance of the legal system. The competition encouraged children to express their thoughts and ideas on legal issues in a creative and engaging way. The program helped to create awareness among children about the legal system and their legal rights. It also helped to build their confidence in expressing their thoughts and ideas on legal issues. The easy writing competition provided an opportunity for children to showcase their creativity and knowledge about legal topics. Through the legal literacy program, LAW Foundation aimed to create a generation of informed and aware citizens who understand their legal rights and responsibilities. The program was a step towards promoting legal literacy among children and empowering them to be active participants in the legal system.

Advocacy and Networking

By the use of networks, campaigns, publications, and open forums, our advocacy and networking programme increases public awareness. We advocate to national lawmakers and decision-makers for the inclusion of gender principles in new and current law and policy.

Research and Impact

To improve the social wellbeing of the community via the support of local research and the implementation of initiatives for public development based on evidence that respect and utilise local resources.
